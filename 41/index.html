26-year-old Kimberly Cerone is getting married. Her fiancé, David Fields, seems like the perfect guy, but is marriage the answer or will it present more questions and surprises that she isn’t up to dealing with? There are just a couple of problems: she’s having second thoughts and the respective in-laws-to-be, an eccentric Irish clan, already can’t stand her outlandish Italian family. She is constantly at odds with Pamela, her kleptomaniac younger sister, and Silvio, her overbearing father, is more concerned with catering to her witch of a stepmother. Flashbacks of her childhood as well as her formative years, including an unusual marriage proposal, depict emotional ups and downs, thus giving a unique glimpse in to her past. With the big day finally here and the ceremony only an hour away, Kimberly begins thinking about how much she wants her independence and separate identity from the dysfunctional family that has looked down upon her ever since her mother passed away. To make matters worse, someone in the wedding entourage is harboring a secret that could ruin everything
